What Are RICS Home Surveys?

RICS Home Surveys are professional inspections designed to assess the condition of a property before purchase. Whether you’re a first-time buyer or an experienced investor, these surveys help you understand potential issues and make informed decisions. The surveys range in depth from a basic condition report to a comprehensive building survey.

Understanding the Survey Levels

Level 1 – RICS Condition Report

Overview:
A Condition Report is the most basic survey offered. It provides a brief overview of the property’s condition without delving into too much detail.

What It Covers:

  • A visual inspection of the property
  • Identification of significant issues that may affect your decision
  • A simple, jargon-free report

Who It’s For:
Ideal for newer properties in generally good condition or for buyers who require only a basic understanding of the property’s state.

Level 2 – RICS HomeBuyer Report

Overview:
The HomeBuyer Report offers a more detailed examination than a Condition Report. It not only identifies defects but also highlights potential future risks and provides advice on repairs.

What It Covers:

  • A detailed visual inspection of the accessible areas
  • An assessment of the property’s condition including potential problem areas
  • Clear recommendations and advice on repair priorities

Who It’s For:
Best suited for conventional properties that are in reasonable condition but may require some remedial work. It provides a balanced overview of both the condition and the market value.

Level 3 – RICS Building Survey

Overview:
The Building Survey is the most comprehensive type of survey available. It involves an in-depth analysis of the property, with a detailed inspection of all accessible parts.

What It Covers:

  • A thorough examination of the structure and fabric of the building
  • Detailed reports on defects, deterioration, and any other factors that could affect the property’s value
  • A comprehensive set of recommendations for repairs and maintenance

Who It’s For:
Ideal for older or unconventional properties, or where significant defects are expected. If you’re purchasing a property that might need extensive work, this survey is the most appropriate choice.

How to Choose the Right Survey

Choosing the right survey depends on several factors, including the age, condition, and type of property you are considering:

  • Property Age and Condition: For newer properties or those in good condition, a Level 1 Condition Report might suffice. However, if you’re considering a property with a mix of old and new features or potential hidden issues, a Level 2 HomeBuyer Report provides greater detail.
  • Extent of Work Needed: If you’re buying an older home or one with obvious signs of wear and tear, a Level 3 Building Survey will offer a comprehensive assessment and help you budget for any future repairs.
  • Your Investment Strategy: Consider your long-term plans for the property. A more detailed survey can provide peace of mind and can be invaluable for future maintenance and renovation planning.
